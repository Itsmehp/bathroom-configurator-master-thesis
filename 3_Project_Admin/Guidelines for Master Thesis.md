# 2. Formal Guidelines

### Contents of a Master Thesis
A master thesis includes the following sections in order:
1.  Title Page
2.  Blank page
3.  Confidentiality Clause (optional)
4.  Gender equality statement
5.  Executive Summary
6.  Table of Contents (including page numbers)
7.  List of Figures (optional)
8.  List of Tables (optional)
9.  List of Abbreviations (optional)
10. Preface (optional)
11. Text (introduction, main body, conclusion)
12. Bibliography
13. Appendix (e.g., questionnaires, interview transcripts)
14. Declaration of Authenticity

---

## 2.1 Title Page
The title page must include the following, centered:
-   **Title** (16 pt, bold)
-   [Subtitle] (14 pt, bold)
-   **"Master Thesis"** (14 pt, bold)
-   **"at Hof University"** (12 pt, bold)
-   **submitted to:** Title, Family Name, and Address (12 pt, bold)
-   **submitted by:** Given Name, Family Name, and Address (12 pt, bold)
-   **Hof, Month, Day, Year** (12 pt, bold)

**Margins:**
-   **Upper:** ca. 10 cm
-   **Lower:** ca. 5.5 cm
-   **Left & Right:** at least 3 cm each

---

## 2.2 Confidentiality Clause (optional)
If the thesis contains confidential data, this clause must be included in the paper and attached to the front cover.

> This bachelor/master thesis contains confidential data of *[company name]*. This work may only be made available to the first and second reviewers and authorized members of the board of examiners. Any publication and duplication of this master thesis - even in part - is prohibited. An inspection of this work by third parties requires the expressed permission of the author and the company *[company name]*.

---

## 2.3 Gender Equality
Gender equality is highly expected. For readability, the masculine spelling is used, but female and other gender identities are explicitly included where necessary.

---

## 2.4 Executive Summary
A concise (approx. 1 page) overview of the thesis. It should contain:
-   A short problem statement.
-   A brief description of the structure and procedure.
-   A condensed summary of the findings.

---

## 2.5 Table of Contents
Provides an overview of all parts of the thesis with page numbers.

---

## 2.6 List of Figures
A chronological list of all illustrations, maps, charts, and graphs, numbered with Arabic numerals.

---

## 2.7 List of Tables
A list of all tabular information, following the same format as the List of Figures.

---

## 2.8 List of Abbreviations
An overview of uncommon abbreviations. Does not need to include common ones like "i.e.", "e.g.", or "cf.".

---

## 2.9 Preface (optional)
May contain personal remarks, acknowledgements, and thanks.
*Note: In Germany, it is not recommended to thank the supervising professor.*
The preface ends with the location, date, and author's full name.

---

## 2.10 Text

### 2.10.1 Contents & Structure
The text consists of three main parts:

-   **Introduction (1 chapter):**
    -   Problem statement & context.
    -   Purpose of the thesis.
    -   Preview of the overall structure.
-   **Main body (3-5 chapters):**
    -   Logical organization of arguments and findings.
    -   Substantiation and accurate documentation.
-   **Conclusion (1 chapter):**
    -   Restatement of developments.
    -   Discussion of important findings.
    -   Derivation of conclusions and future outlook.

### 2.10.2 Layout
-   **Fonts:** Times New Roman / Arial (be consistent).
-   **Font size:** 12 pt.
-   **Line spacing:** 1.5-spaced.
-   **Arrangement:** Full justification (block).
-   **Paragraphs:** Single blank line afterwards.
-   **Margins:**
    -   **Left:** 3.0 cm
    -   **Right:** 2.0 cm
    -   **Upper:** 3.0 cm
    -   **Lower:** 3.0 cm
-   **Numeration:**
    -   Text pages use Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3...).
    -   Preliminary pages use Roman numerals (i, ii, iii...). The title page is "I" but the number is not shown.

### 2.10.3 Headings
-   Start level-1 headings on a new page.
-   Use bold font style.
-   **Level-1 headings:** 16pt
-   **Level-2 headings:** 14pt
-   **Level-3 & 4 headings:** 12pt

### 2.10.4 Visual Information (Figures & Tables)
-   All figures and tables must be labeled and numbered.
-   **Tables:** Number and title go *above*.
-   **Figures:** Number and title go *below*.
-   Sources are indicated in footnotes. Self-made figures are labeled "Own illustration based on...".
-   You must describe the figure/table in the text and explain its main message.

### 2.10.5 Quotation & Citation
All information from other publications must be cited twice: once in the text (short reference) and once in the bibliography (detailed reference).

-   **Direct Quotation:** Use quotation marks `“...”` and a footnote.
-   **Indirect Quotation / Paraphrase:** Rephrase in your own words and add a footnote.

#### Footnotes
-   Use superscript numbers (¹, ², ³).
-   **Format:** `Author’s last name (Year of publication), p. XX`
-   **Font:** Times New Roman / Arial, 10pt, single-spaced.

#### Additional Citation Guidelines
-   **< 4 Authors:** List all last names.
-   **> 3 Authors:** Use `et al.` (e.g., `Author’s last name et al. (Year of publication), p. XX`).
-   **Multiple works, same author, same year:** Add a letter (a, b, c) after the year (e.g., `(2023a)`).
-   **Successive quotes from the same source:** Use `(ibid.)`.

---

## 2.11 Bibliography
-   Lists detailed references for all cited sources.
-   Do not include sources that were not cited.
-   List sources in ascending alphabetical order.

---

## 2.12 Appendix
-   Contains supplementary data (e.g., questionnaires, interview transcripts).
-   Each appendix should be clearly separated with a capital letter (A, B, C...).

---

## 2.13 Declaration of Authenticity
> I certify that this thesis does not incorporate without acknowledgement any material previously submitted for a degree or diploma in any university; and that to the best of my knowledge and belief it does not contain any material previously published or written by another person where due reference is not made in the text.
